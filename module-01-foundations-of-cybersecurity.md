## Core Concepts

- **Cybersecurity:** Protecting systems, networks, devices, people, and data from unauthorized access, disruption, damage, or theft.
- **Information security:** Protecting information in all forms, including digital, physical, and verbal information.
- **Security posture:** How prepared an organization is to prevent, detect, respond to, and recover from threats.
- **Threat:** Anything that can cause harm.
- **Vulnerability:** A weakness that can be exploited.
- **Risk:** The possibility of harm when a threat can exploit a vulnerability.
- **Security control:** A safeguard used to reduce risk.

## CIA Triad

- **Confidentiality:** Only authorized people can access information.
- **Integrity:** Information remains accurate, trustworthy, and unchanged unless authorized.
- **Availability:** Systems and data are accessible when needed.

## Security Roles

- **Security Analyst:** Reviews alerts, risks, logs, reports, and controls.
- **SOC Analyst:** Monitors security events and triages alerts.
- **Incident Responder:** Investigates, contains, and documents incidents.
- **GRC Analyst:** Works with governance, risk, compliance, policies, and controls.
- **Security Engineer:** Builds and maintains security systems and controls.

## Frameworks, Controls, and Governance

- **Framework:** Organized guidance for managing security work.
- **Control:** A safeguard that reduces risk.
- **Governance:** How security decisions, responsibilities, and policies are managed.
- **Compliance:** Following required laws, standards, contracts, or internal policies.

Common examples to recognize:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- NIST Risk Management Framework
- CIS Controls
- ISO standards
- PCI DSS
- HIPAA
- GDPR
- SOC 1 and SOC 2 reports

## Ethics in Cybersecurity

Security professionals must act legally, protect privacy, avoid unauthorized access, and handle sensitive information responsibly. Curiosity is useful, but security work must stay within permission and policy.

## Common Tools

- **SIEM:** Collects and analyzes security logs.
- **IDS/IPS:** Detects or blocks suspicious network activity.
- **Antivirus/EDR:** Helps detect malicious activity on endpoints.
- **Playbook:** Documented steps for responding to a specific situation.
- **Command-line interface:** Text-based way to interact with systems.
- **Database:** Organized data storage that can be searched or queried.

## Quick Review

- Cybersecurity protects confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
- Risk comes from the relationship between assets, threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, and impact.
- Security work uses people, process, technology, policy, and communication.
- Frameworks help organize security programs.
- Ethics and authorization are required in all cybersecurity work.
